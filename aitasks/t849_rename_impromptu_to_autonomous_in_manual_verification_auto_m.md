## Context

The recently-landed manual-verification auto-execution feature (t843)
introduced the term **`impromptu`** as a strategy / profile-value alongside
`prebuilt`. The term is used in:

- **Profile key** `manual_verification_auto_mode` accepts
  `ask | never | impromptu | prebuilt_approve | prebuilt_autorun`
- **Auto-Verification Procedure** `strategy` parameter: `"impromptu" | "prebuilt"`
- **User-facing AskUserQuestion** option label: "Yes, impromptu (Recommended)"

`impromptu` is too exotic an English word for an enum value that AI agents
and human users both have to read and recognize. The intended meaning is:
*the agent is free to improvise — it autonomously picks the verification
approach per item on the fly, executes it, and only reports a-posteriori
in the plan file.*

After weighing alternatives (`improvised`, `freeform`, `inline`, `autonomous`,
`adhoc`), **`autonomous`** was chosen — it directly emphasizes the
agent-autonomy aspect (free to decide per item without an up-front
approved plan), pairs cleanly with `prebuilt`, and is familiar AI/agent
terminology.

## Goal

Rename `impromptu` → `autonomous` across the manual-verification
auto-execution feature, including the profile enum value, the
`strategy` procedure parameter, the user-facing prompt option, all
descriptive prose, and rendered/golden artifacts.
